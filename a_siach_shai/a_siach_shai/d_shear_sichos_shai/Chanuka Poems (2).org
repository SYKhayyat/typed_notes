Hi! Here are the different parts of your kuntres arranged into several poems. The poems do change in rhythm and rhyme, so keep that in mind when reading them. Feel free to edit them, but please run by me any edit that changes the rhythm, rhyme, or alliteration.
1.
Very long before the neis,
something drastic did take place:
Sensing they could not withstand
avodah zara’s outstretched hand,
the Rabbis, yelling, ”בייא בייא בייא,“
made its yetzer hara die.
They punctured its pernicious pull,
and prayed until its death was full.
Distanced so from something bad,
prophecy could not be had;
except for Torah shebal peh,
maintaining zeh le’umas zeh.
2.
Chochmah yevanis, a wisdom of sorts,
with brilliant deductions, and falling just short
of chochmas haTorah, was born around then,
preaching a faith in the powers of men.
They only believed what the mind could achieve,
and they rest they would leave as complete make-believe.
Although excellent thinkers, they grasped not the truth-
that the Torah is emmes, and needs not the proof
of a curious mind or the senses of man;
for we still must believe what we can’t understand.
3.
Thus began a struggle ‘tween opposing schools of thought-
the Jews, who kept the Torah; the Yevanim, who did not.
The Greeks soon made gezeiros ‘gainst our yachas to Hashem,
and, quite confused, some Jews would choose, to be a part of them.
And, as they watched their faithful leave, and watched the Greek school grow,
the Rabbis knew just what to do; the Greeks would have to go.
A couple weak and wizened sages, hardly trained to fight,
faced the forces of the Greeks, despite their frightful might.
The Chashmonaim’s victory soon proved the Torah right,
and quickly banished Yavan’s dark, before the Torah’s light.
But lacking was the tahor oil used to light up bright
the golden tall menorah the kohanim lit each night;
for all of it was tamei now, since midivrei chachamim,
the Greeks who touched the oil all were zavos and were zavim.
And, thus, they could not use the oil, but received a neis-
the finding of a jug of oil that would burn eight days.
4.
The Greeks are gone, but what lives on
gives a cause for grief.
Their thinkers still, and always will,
challenge our belief.
The Torah looks like any book
filled with earthly wisdom;
we have to know that it has, though,
holiness that’s hidden.
And, as we learn, the schar we earn
brings Hashem’s hashpah;
so let us strive to energize
our limud haTorah.
5.
Past events control the present;
time repeats itself.
Thus, a special strength from Heaven
every year is sent
on the days that, long ago,
these events occurred,
allowing us to learn and grow;
it would be absurd
to squander such a splendid gift,
lasting eight days long-
which is what will happen if
it is spent all wrong.
6.
The mitzvos are three:
The candles we see,
The Hallel we say,
Al Hanissim we pray.
Their reason’s the same:
to thank Hashem’s name;
so prepare soon to start
to connect in your heart
to Hashem when we pray,
and light every day.
7.
To finish off these little lines,
remember these two points,
and far after the candle dies,
you will feel its warmth.
1. The yetzer hara tries each way
2. to get us all to sin.
3. We fight against him every day,
4. and hopefully we win.
5. Even when he seems too strong
6. for us to defeat,
7. know that such a thought is wrong;
8. the strong lost to the weak.
9. Gratitude is number two-
10. feel it in your heart.
11. Think it through, and say it too,
12. and speak out every part.
