To The Tune of: Chanukah, Oh Chanukah
A yom tov, a moed
Is not just a feast day-
Not just a party,
A feel-right-at-ease day.

Every yom tov brings
A kedushah and hashpaah
An imprint of the nes
That our nation saw.

Chorus:
For time is a circle,
Revolving each day in the year;
It spins like a dreidel,1
and that’s why we’re able
To bring its hashra’ah so near.
End of Chorus.

Chanukah is tricky,
To keep our attention-
For eight nights we try to
Keep up the momentum.

Focus on the mitzvah,
Think and strategize;
Do it with a freshness
That makes it come alive.

Chorus.

When the Yavanim
Tried hard to change us,
Our holy chachamim
With nissim did save us.

Bringing back the Torah,
Avodas Hashem;
Now that same hashpaah
Is present once again.

Chorus.

If not for the nissim,
that saved our mesorah,
We would have been lost,
Have forgotten our Torah.

Chanukah’s a time 
For special Torah growth
That’s why the yetzer hara
Tries to stop it so.

Chorus.

To The Tune of: Modim (Baruch Levine)
Chanukah’s unique
Among our holidays;
We act in other ways
Than other moadim.

All melachah is allowed-
No restrictions placed-
For our avodah’s based on thanking Him.

Chorus:
We thank Him for the neis
That long ago took place,
For it brought us close to Him;
The Torah that we learn,
And all the schar we earn-
We thank Hashem for all these things.
End of Chorus.

The chanukas hamizbeach,
Avodah was restored
Korbanos brought once more-
We sing so thankfully!

A return to Yiddishkeit,
Mitzvos once again-
Closer to Hashem we’ll always be.

Chorus.

If not for this return to Him,
We would never be
Ultimately free
From physical desire.

We’re eternal and will get
Much eternal schar,
And forever are growing higher.

Chorus.

The mitzvos that we do these days
show our thanks to Him-
the hallel that we sing,
and al hanissim,

lighting the menorah
as pirsum haneis
is another way of thanking Him.

It’s hard to give hodaah.

Time and time again,
We have to thank hashem-
Everything comes straight from him-
Thank you hashem!
End of Chorus.

Creating and sustaining.
All comes from shamayim;
All comes straight from him.

Then take those feeling and sing his praise,
Repay hashem hashem with your thank you;
Thank him in a specific way
For each and every thing.

To The Tune of: Min Hameitzar (Kol Salonika)
Just like the yevanim fought hard to destroy
The kdusha and the tahara of every Jewish boy,
So too in our lifetime, we have a foe to win:
The yetzer hara’s trying to snag us up in sin.

Sheva yipol tzadik, but get up when you fall;
The fight against the yetzer is the hardest fight of all.
For no one is born perfect, and we must fight our way
Closer to Hashem, every single day.

Chorus:-
We’re soldiers who are fighting a very trying war
We never can surrender; we’ll fight for evermore!
We know we’re often hopeless, but once we do our best,
Hashem will fight our battles, Hashem will do the rest.
End of Chorus.

The Jews who fought yevanim were scrawny and were weak,
Living in the caves where they had little food to eat;
Fighting  the yevanim, who always emphasized
Mighty muscled bodies and proper exercise.

But they did their hishtadlus, Hashem made sure they won;
Hashem will do the fighting, once our part is done.
When the yetzer hara seems too tough to beat,
Remember that Hashem is planning its defeat.

Chorus.

The yetzer hara tells us that we’re so far and weak,
It tells us to desert war, to beat a quick retreat.
But, even when we’re distant, straying from Hashem.
Hashem says keep on fighting- he’ll make you close again.

Kol haba litaher misayim oso-
One who comes to purify, Hashem will help him grow:
Walk into the elevator, in a bit of time,
you will soar up higher than you knew to climb.

Chorus.
