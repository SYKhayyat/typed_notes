* Ludicrously Laughable Lomdus Machine
* Like a car that does not drive
* or a corpse that’s come alive;
* like a used Gemara Nida or a bee that does not hum.
* Like a Ketzos that is not shver
* or Reb Shimmy with no hair,
* a bachur felt in lomdus will always be a bum.
* lomdus is the way we think and how we kriech a pshtat,
* ‘cause lomdus tells us what just what we derher.
* So if you want to feel what’s right and handel with the best,
* just spend first-seder on Rav Baruch Ber.
* like a chicken without eggs
* or a bench with broken legs;
* like a taaneh that’s a taaneh that’s taane’ing fakhert
* Like a shiur without a hock
* or a balebatish pshat,
* a bachur nisht mit lomdus can’t know what he derhers
* Lomdus is…
* like an ice-cold lemonade
* or a shiur mit gantze reid
* like a bustling beis medrash or kol toirah you can feel
* like the first day of the zman
* or nedarim mit de ran
* a bachur who learns lomdus is a bochur who’s for real
* Lomdus is…
One fish two fish red fish blue fish
tall fish short fish old fish new fish.
Have you ever seen the fish
called the fearsome Shalom Spitz?
Is fangs are sharp, his eyes are keen;
he always yells, “Bentzeeyn, Bentzeeyn!”
We brought him home once as a pet.
We do not feel quite better yet.
Here’s a fish our father found.
He’s very old and very round.
He has a head that’s full of hair
(we try our utmost not to stare).
He sits and snoozzes on the ground,
and twirls his glasses round and round.
We find his presence very calming;
I think that we shall call him Kalman.
Oh me! Oh my! Egad! Oh no!
I think this pet will have to go!
Good grief! Gevalt! Oy vey! Oh dear!
I think there won’t be morning shiur![fn:1]
* Footnotes
[fn:1] This was written after I started a trend of announcing canceled shiurim in rhyme. This fit to the suggested title of “one shiur two shiur, red shiur no shiur.”
