* The Ballad of Elchonon and Devorah
* Along the street, there strolls someone,
* Aged hardly more than twenty-one,
* Smiling shyly, dressed his best,
* For his love, Devorah Tress.
* He spots her and begins to stare-
* She’s beautiful beyond compare!-
* Floating in a purple dress,
* His love, Devorah Tress.
* In eager steps, the lovers walk;
* In hushed delight, they softly talk;
* Enraptured, he cannot suppress
* His love for Devorah Tress.
* For many weeks, they’d briefly meet
* On quiet darkened lonely streets-
* He’d never dreamed his friends would guess
* His love for Devorah Tress.
* His friends and teachers told him no-
* They told him she would have to go-
* With painful words which strain and test
* His love for Devorah Tress.
* He asked his parents desp’rately,
* Tense with hope that they’d agree;
* Alas! They could not give a yes
* To his love, Devorah Tress.
* One rainy day, a letter came:
* “My conscience says I can’t remain-
* I cannot cause you such distress
- your love, Devorah Tress.”
* His tears fell freely on the floor-
* How could he live now anymore?!-
* He mourned for her, his heart a mess,
* For his love, Devorah Tress.
* But time flowed on, despite his plights;
* It brought him joys and mad delights-
* But none could match the soft caress
* Of his love, Devorah Tress.
